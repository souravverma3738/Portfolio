Napier-Bank Messaging Application

The Napier Bank Messaging System, developed as part of the software engineering coursework at Edinburgh Napier University, is an advanced application designed to manage diverse communication channels within the banking environment. This system facilitates the efficient handling of SMS, emails, and Tweets, allowing users, employees, and managers to create inquiries and post updates seamlessly. SMS messages processed by the system are restricted to 140 characters and may include textspeak abbreviations, which are automatically expanded to their full forms to ensure clarity and professionalism. Email functionality is robust, supporting both standard emails and Significant Incident Reports (SIRs) from branch managers. SIRs follow a structured format, detailing the branch sort code and the nature of the incident, thus providing consistent and thorough documentation of serious events. For security, embedded URLs in emails are quarantined and replaced with placeholders.
